Timeline project

Exploratory project - reconstructing a personal timeline (places and countries visited, activities, and events) using nothing but metadata from smartphone photos.

The project demonstrates how passive data captured by everyday devices can be transformed into a compelling geospatial narrative.

It visualizes travel patterns across countries, cities, and neighborhoods, revealing a unique story of movement and experience through time and space.

About This Project

Modern photos automatically contain EXIF metadata, including the creation timestamp and GPS coordinates. This project explores whether that data alone is enough to rebuild a meaningful record of where someone has been and when.

The app processes a collection of photos, extracts their timestamp and geolocation metadata, and transforms the data into an interactive timeline mapped onto the world. Users can scroll through dates and instantly see where photos were taken, revealing travel patterns across countries, cities, and neighborhoods.

The interface pairs a chronological activity feed with a dynamic map, allowing users to explore their movement history day-by-day. At a global scale, the visualization shows travel clusters across continents, while zooming into specific regions reveals dense city-level photo activity.
